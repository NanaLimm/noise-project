Noticeable Impact of Noise on Learning

Interpretation of the Result

Your results indicate a noticeable negative impact of noise on learning efficiency. At this level, noise is likely to reduce concentration, slow down task completion, and increase mental fatigue. Unlike the “hidden impact” level, the influence of noise here is often recognised by the student (for example, feeling distracted or irritated).

What you may experience

  • More frequent loss of attention and the need to re-focus.
  • Slower reading speed and weaker comprehension in noisy places.
  • Higher mental effort required to complete normal academic tasks.
  • Increased irritability or tension during study sessions.

Detailed Recommendations

  • Choose quieter locations: library, study room, or a space with minimal conversations and unpredictable sounds.
  • Use strong noise reduction: active noise-cancelling headphones or earplugs can be effective at this level.
  • Structure your study: use time blocks (e.g., 25–30 minutes work + 5 minutes break) to reduce cognitive overload.
  • Prioritise deep-focus tasks in silence: reading, writing, and problem-solving should be done under the best conditions.
  • Separate tasks by difficulty: do easier tasks (review, flashcards) in slightly noisier places, and complex tasks in quiet.
  • Reduce sudden distractions: turn off notifications, close extra tabs, and minimise visual clutter on the desk.
  • Monitor fatigue: if tiredness increases rapidly, shorten sessions and increase recovery time.
