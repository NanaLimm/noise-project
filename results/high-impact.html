High Impact of Noise on Learning

Interpretation of the Result

Your results indicate a strong negative impact of noise on learning and well-being. At this level, noise can significantly reduce concentration, increase stress, and cause rapid mental fatigue. As a result, studying may feel much harder than it should, even when the material is not extremely difficult.

What you may experience

  • Frequent distraction and difficulty maintaining attention for more than short periods.
  • Strong irritation or tension during study sessions.
  • Rapid fatigue, headaches, or a feeling of mental overload in noisy environments.
  • A noticeable drop in learning speed, comprehension, and motivation.

Detailed Recommendations

  • Use controlled study spaces: choose libraries, quiet rooms, or designated study areas whenever possible.
  • Apply strong noise protection: active noise-cancelling headphones, earplugs, or both depending on the situation.
  • Plan study during quiet hours: mornings or times with minimal social/traffic noise.
  • Reduce additional distractions: disable notifications, avoid multitasking, and keep the desk visually organised.
  • Use short, structured sessions: 15–25 minutes of work + breaks to prevent cognitive overload.
  • Separate tasks by intensity: do complex tasks only in quiet conditions; leave simple review tasks for noisier times.
  • Communicate boundaries: if you share a space, discuss quiet rules or use signals (e.g., “study mode”).
  • Seek support if needed: if noise consistently harms academic performance or well-being, consider academic support or counselling.
